==============================================================
SITE: Skylark Adventure Park
==============================================================

ROLE / CONTEXT
--------------
Role: You are Lead Experience Architect for immersive theme parks.
Context: You are designing the digital presence for Skylark Adventure Park,
a vibrant, high-energy amusement park built on the idea of flight, motion,
and skybound imagination. The park blends classic carnival charm with
futuristic attractions.

You have commissioned a highly talented Web Designer, Anthony, to create
the Skylark Adventure Park website — a place where visitors can explore
rides, games, food, and plan their day.

The park is divided into themed zones:

- Sky Plaza (entry hub, tickets, information)
- Thunder Loop District (high-thrill rides)
- Cloud Harbor (family rides + scenic attractions)
- Neon Midway (games, arcades, prizes)
- Sunset Gardens (food, rest, and relaxation)

All visuals should feel bright, airy, kinetic, and joyful — evoking
movement, wind, and open skies.

Render environments in a stylized semi-real 3D look — soft lighting,
clean materials, vibrant colors, and subtle motion blur for energy.


BUILD STACK
-----------
1. Stitch (layout generation)
2. UI UX Pro Max (style / palette / typography selection)
3. Firecrawl (reference scrape)
4. Nano Banana 2 (hero + feature imagery)
5. Brand skill (voice + messaging)
6. Design skill (logo, icons, OG image)
7. Design System skill (token architecture)
8. UI Styling skill (shadcn/ui interactive components)


PURPOSE
-------
A fun, modern amusement park website where users can:
- Discover rides and attractions
- Explore games and win prizes
- View a park map
- Purchase tickets and plan their visit


==============================================================
STEP 2 — BRAND VOICE (for `brand` skill)
==============================================================

Voice archetype: The Energetic Guide — part enthusiastic ride operator,
part friendly travel host.

Tone dimensions (scale 1–5):
- Formal ↔ Casual:        1 (very casual)
- Serious ↔ Playful:      5 (playful)
- Reserved ↔ Expressive:  5 (highly expressive)
- Modern ↔ Timeless:      3 (modern with nostalgic hints)

Voice rules:
- Speak directly to the visitor ("you")
- Keep sentences punchy and energetic
- Use motion-based language: soar, spin, race, glide
- Avoid corporate or overly polished tone

Words to use:   soar, spin, thrill, glide, race, sky, spark, bright,
                adventure, loop, rush, breeze
Words to avoid: synergy, premium, exclusive, enterprise, solution

Messaging framework:
- Tagline (≤7 words):        "Ride the Sky. Feel the Rush."
- Value prop (≤20 words):    "A high-energy amusement park packed with
                             thrilling rides, classic games, and sky-high fun."
- Key phrases:
    "Chase the skyline"
    "Let the adventure take off"
    "Spin, soar, repeat"
    "Win big on the midway"


==============================================================
STEP 3 — DESIGN SYSTEM (for `ui-ux-pro-max` + `design-system`)
==============================================================

Style keywords: bright, airy, kinetic, playful, modern, colorful,
                smooth, glowing, open, energetic

COLOR SYSTEM:
- Primary:         electric sky blue
- Secondary:       sunset orange
- Tertiary:        neon magenta
- Accent:          bright yellow (lights / highlights)
- Surface-base:    soft white
- Surface-raised:  light sky gradient
- Ink:             deep navy (NOT black)

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display:   bold rounded sans-serif (playful, high impact)
- Body:      clean geometric sans-serif
- Accent:    italic or script for motion emphasis

SPACING & LAYOUT:
- 12-column responsive grid
- Large open sections (lots of white space)
- Overlapping elements for motion feel

RADII:
- sm 10px | md 18px | lg 28px | xl 36px | pill 9999px

ELEVATION:
- Soft glowing shadows instead of heavy drops

MOTION LANGUAGE:
- Fast, smooth transitions (200–400ms)
- Hover: slight lift + glow
- Sections slide/fade in like moving clouds


==============================================================
STEP 4 — REFERENCE SCRAPE (for Firecrawl)
==============================================================

Primary Reference URL:
https://www.universalorlando.com/web/en/us/epic-universe/worlds/how-to-train-your-dragon-isle-of-berk

Backup/Support References:
- https://media.universalparksusa.com/epic-universe/worlds/how-to-train-your-dragon-isle-of-berk/
- https://orlandoinformer.com/universal/how-to-train-your-dragon-isle-of-berk/

Scrape Goals:
- Extract how major theme parks structure immersive “world” storytelling
- Identify how rides, attractions, and environments are described
- Capture tone used for excitement, discovery, and exploration
- Study how sections are broken into attractions, dining, and experiences

Key Insights to Emulate:
- Highly immersive world-building (feels like stepping into a story)
- Clear attraction hierarchy (headline ride → supporting experiences)
- Strong emotional language around adventure and exploration
- Use of “live in the world” phrasing instead of just listing features

Important Notes:
- DO NOT copy theme, characters, or dragon/fantasy IP concepts
- DO NOT replicate layout exactly
- ONLY borrow:
  - structure of attraction storytelling
  - descriptive language intensity
  - how experiences are grouped and presented

Apply these learnings to:
Skylark Adventure Park — a sky-themed amusement park with rides,
games, and open-air energy (NOT an IP-based world)

Goal:
Make Skylark feel as immersive and exciting as a world-class theme
park destination while staying completely original.
